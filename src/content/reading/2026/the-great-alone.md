_The Great Alone_ was an emotional rollercoaster. A mother-daughter novel and a modern
Romeo and Juliet love story wrapped up in a beautifully described setting. Hannah makes
Alaska feel like another planet compared to the Lower 48. The portrayals of nature
reminded me most of Kim Stanley Robinson in _Red Mars_, which is one of my favorite books
of all time.

There were slower parts, and parts where I was frustrated by how much the protagonist kept
losing over and over again. But the volume of tears that I shed by the end proves that it
was all worth it in the end.
